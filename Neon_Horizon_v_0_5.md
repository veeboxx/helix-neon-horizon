# HELIX: NEON HORIZON

**A Cyberpunk Tabletop RPG for Two Players**

**Version 0.5: Alpha Playtest**

---

# READ THIS FIRST

HELIX: Neon Horizon is a cyberpunk tabletop RPG for two players. There is no Game Master. You and your partner share the world.

**How it works:** One player is the driver. The driver frames scenes, sets the pace, and moves the story forward. They say "this happened, so now this." The other player pushes back. They voice NPCs, narrate what goes wrong when the dice turn against you, and ask hard questions. They say "this happened, but the ground shifts." Halfway through the session, you swap roles. Next session, swap who goes first.

**Both players always play their own characters.** The driver/push-back roles only determine who controls the world around the characters. You never lose control of your character.

**The dice say "no."** Neither player is the antagonist. The dice and the Consequence Tables decide when the world pushes back. Your partner interprets what that push-back looks like. You are collaborators telling a story about a world that fights you, not two people fighting each other.

**The fiction is the game.** "The fiction" means the shared imaginary space: the story you are building together. There is no Meridian Sprawl until someone describes the rain hitting the flooded street. There is no danger until someone says the lights just went out. When the rules say "the fiction demands it," they mean does it make sense in the story you are telling?

**Drama first.** If the answer is obvious and dramatic, just say it. Do not roll. Most questions have an answer both players can feel. Roll when the outcome is genuinely uncertain. Ask the Oracle when you are stuck. Maximum three Oracle calls per scene.

An Oracle system fills the gaps when neither of you knows what happens next. The dice, the Oracles, and your shared imagination generate the fiction as you play.

**One session, one mission.** Each session follows the HELIX cycle: Hook, Explore, Leverage, Ignite, Exhale. 75 to 105 minutes. One complete arc.

# CHAPTER 1: ON STORYTELLING

A story told alone is a closed loop. One mind, one voice, one vision. The storyteller knows the ending before the audience does. It is beautiful, but it is a monologue dressed in costume.

Collaborative storytelling is something else entirely.

When two people sit across from each other and build a fiction together, something happens that neither of them controls. You say a name and it becomes real. You describe a room and it exists. The fiction is not written. It is spoken into being. The act of saying it makes it true.

And because there are two of you, the creation is ungovernable. You cannot predict what your partner will say. You say "the server room is empty" and your partner says "except for the body." Now there is a body. You did not put it there. They did. And now you both have to deal with it.

The best moments in play are the ones nobody authored. They emerged from a cause meeting a complication in a way that neither player saw coming. That is the feeling. That is what you are chasing.

You are not playing a game. You are not performing a story. You are having a story. the way you have a conversation, the way you have a meal, the way you have a fight. It is happening to both of you at the same time and neither of you is in charge and that is exactly why it works.

Speak. Listen. Build. Complicate. The fiction is not on the page. It is in the air between you.

### The Therefore/However Principle

Every beat connects to the next in one of two ways:

**Therefore**. cause and effect. This happened, so now this.

**However**. complication. This happened, but the ground shifts.

If your story connects with "and then". nobody cares. There is no tension. Just sequence.

### Drama First

If the answer is obvious and dramatic, just say it. Do not roll. Do not look it up.

1. Obvious? Say it. Move on.
2. Uncertain? Roll a Check.
3. Stuck? Ask the Oracle. Maximum three Oracle calls per scene.

# CHAPTER 2: THE HELIX CYCLE

## How Two Players Share the World

One player drives. The other pushes back.

The driver frames scenes, sets the pace, and moves the fiction forward. They say "therefore". this happened, so now this.

The player pushing back plays the world resisting. They voice NPCs, narrate what goes wrong on Twists and Misses, and ask hard questions. They say "however". this happened, but the ground shifts.

The dice say "no." Neither player is the antagonist. The dice and the Consequence Tables decide when the world pushes back. Your partner interprets what that push-back looks like.

Both players always play their own characters. These roles only determine who controls the world around the characters.

At the halfway point. the Leverage phase. you swap. Next session, swap who starts driving.

## The Five Phases

### H. HOOK (5 minutes)

The driver states what happened and why it matters. The other player asks one question that makes it worse.

If you do not have a Hook, use the Job Generator. If last session's Exhale left an obvious thread, pull it. The Hook is a door. Walk through it.

### Pre-Mission Setup

Before the first Explore scene:

1. **Choose an Approach:** Stealth, Social, Assault, Technical, or Deception. This determines which Consequence Table applies to the first scene.
2. **Roll Engagement (d6):**

| d6 | Position |
| -- | -------- |
| 1 | Desperate. Threat Clock starts at 2. |
| 2-3 | Risky. Clocks at 0. |
| 4-5 | Controlled. +1 Momentum bonus. |
| 6 | Dominant. First Check is automatic Full Hit. |

3. **Prep Checks:** Each character makes one Prep Check (any Attribute + Tag). Momentum earned goes into the shared pool. See Momentum in Chapter 2.

### E. EXPLORE (20-30 minutes, 1-2 scenes)

The driver frames scenes and pushes forward. The other player provides opposition and consequences.

**Scene Framing. four lines:**
1. **Shot:** One sentence. location and sensory detail.
2. **Cast:** Who is present. PCs and 0-2 NPCs.
3. **Pressure:** What goes wrong if nobody acts.
4. **Question:** What does this scene answer or decide.

When nothing new is happening, cut. Narrate the transition in one sentence.

By the end of Explore, both players should know: what the objective really is, what stands in the way, and what Clock is ticking.

### L. LEVERAGE (15-20 minutes, 1 scene)

**You swap.** The player who was pushing back now drives. The Leverage is the biggest "however" of the session. Something shifts that the other player did not see coming. It is not a consequence of failure. It fires regardless. The world moved while you were busy.

### I. IGNITE (20-30 minutes, 1-2 scenes)

The climax. Everything converges. The driver pushes toward the objective. The player pushing back throws everything at them. Momentum gets burned. Consequences are not pulled.

### X. EXHALE (15-20 minutes)

Both players. No roles. Work through the post-mission sequence together.

**Exhale Sequence:**

1. **Mission Burden Check.** Count factors. Roll d6 per factor. Each 5+ is +1 Burden.
2. **Economic Shocks.** Roll shock dice based on Burden band. Resolve shocks.
3. **Connection Strain.** Any Connections at 4/4 Strain must be resolved now (see Chapter 6).
4. **Faction Turn.** For each faction involved in the mission, roll on the Faction Turn table.
5. **Pay.** If the job paid, reduce Burden accordingly.
6. **Downtime Actions.** Each character gets 2 downtime actions (minus Burden tax: -1 action at Drowning, -2 at Breaking Point). Choose from the Downtime Menu.
7. **Drive Progress.** Tick segments based on what happened this session.
8. **Flags.** Each player names one thing they want challenged and one thing they want to see next session.

### Faction Turn Table (d6)

For each faction that was involved in or affected by the mission, roll d6:

| d6 | Result | What Happens |
| -- | ------ | ------------ |
| 1 | Retreat | Faction pulls back. Loses ground. Their Clock ticks backward by 1. |
| 2 | Hold | No change. They are watching. |
| 3 | Pressure | Faction leans on someone. Roll d6: 1-3 targets an NPC, 4-6 targets a player Connection (+1 Strain). |
| 4 | Advance | Faction Clock ticks forward by 1. They are making progress on their goal. |
| 5 | Escalate | Clock ticks forward by 1 AND they target someone. roll d6 as Pressure above. |
| 6 | Power Move | Clock ticks forward by 2. Something visible changes in the city. Partner narrates what. |

If a faction's Clock fills, their goal is achieved. The fiction changes permanently. A new Clock may emerge from the consequences.

## Downtime Menu

Each character gets 2 downtime actions. At Burden 6-8, you lose 1 to survival. At Burden 9-10, you lose 2.

| Action | What Happens |
| ------ | ------------ |
| **Side Gig** | Roll NERVE or WITS. Full Hit: -2 Burden. Twist: -1 Burden, but +1 Strain on a Connection or tick a faction Clock (the gig touched someone's territory). Miss: no pay, +1 Burden (you attracted attention). |
| **Recover** | Clear 2 harm boxes across both tracks. Or spend both downtime actions for Full Recovery (clear all). |
| **Tend Connection** | Clear 2 Strain from one Connection. Describe the scene. |
| **Maintain Chrome** | Keep one cyberware augment running. Required at Burden 9-10 or it malfunctions. A malfunctioning augment cannot be activated and its starting ability does not apply. The partner can also narrate glitches, phantom feedback, or involuntary activation as free complications once per scene. A WITS Check during downtime or a visit to a street doc restores function. |
| **Work the Streets** | Gather intel. WITS Check. Full Hit: ask 2 questions about the next job. Twist: 1 question, but someone knows you are asking. Miss: bad intel. partner plants a false detail you will not discover until it matters. |
| **Advance** | Spend toward an augment upgrade or new augment. Requires milestone + fictional justification. |
| **Lay Low** | Do nothing visible. -1 Burden. No other benefit. Sometimes survival is enough. |
| **Redirect Fallout** | Push your problems onto a Connection. -1 Burden, +1 Strain on that Connection. Describe what you asked them to deal with. |

## Pushing Back

When you are pushing back and the Consequence Tables do not fit the moment:

* Separate the characters
* Reveal an unwelcome truth
* Offer a deal with a catch
* Tick a Clock
* Add Strain to a Connection
* Inflict harm (fiction determines severity)
* Introduce a new NPC or faction interest
* Make a Flaw or Scar relevant right now
* Take something away (gear, access, safety)
* Show the cost of Burden (a bill arrives, chrome glitches, credstick declined)

These are not punishments. They are "howevers."

## Crossroads

When the fiction produces an irreversible decision, frame it as a Crossroads. a yes/no question with no take-backs.

The character most affected makes the call. The other player predicts consequences. No roll. The decision is the point.

## Drive Progress and Resolution

Your Drive has an 8-segment Progress Track.

| Achievement | Segments |
| ----------- | -------- |
| Minor (gathered intel, made a connection) | 1 |
| Significant (completed a related mission, overcame a major obstacle) | 2 |
| Breakthrough (major discovery, dramatic sacrifice) | 3 |

**The Drive Check:** When you believe your Drive is within reach, roll d10 against filled segments. Roll equal or under = Resolved. Over by 1-2 = Almost, one more obstacle. Over by 3+ = Path changes.

New Drives connect to something on your sheet. Abandoned Drives: lose all Momentum, start at 0/8.

## Putting a Name to a Face

WITS Check when meeting an NPC for the first time. Tag steps up if connected to their world. Faction Goodwill d8+ steps up.

| Result | What You Know |
| ------ | ------------- |
| Full Hit | Know them. Reputation, faction, one useful detail. |
| Twist | Know OF them. Reputation only. The detail costs you. someone notices you asking. |
| Miss | Never seen them. Or worse. they remember you. |

## Disagreements

If both players disagree about what should happen next, default to the Oracle. Roll d6. Accept the answer. If the disagreement is about tone, content, or boundaries, pause and talk. The Oracle resolves fiction disputes. Human conversations resolve everything else.

# THE DIE STEP LADDER

**d6 → d8 → d10 → d12**

* Step UP: move right. d8 becomes d10.
* Step DOWN: move left. d8 becomes d6.
* Multiple steps are cumulative.
* Cannot step above d12. Cannot step below d6 (die auto-fails).

# CHAPTER 3: CHARACTER CREATION

## Step 1: Concept

One sentence. Who are you?

## Step 2: Attributes

Assign d10, d8, d8, d6 to GUTS, EDGE, WITS, NERVE.

| Attribute | Covers |
| --------- | ------ |
| GUTS | Force, endurance, toughness, courage |
| EDGE | Speed, reflexes, precision, stealth |
| WITS | Perception, knowledge, tech, hacking |
| NERVE | Composure, willpower, social instinct, deception |

Variants: Gritty (d8, d8, d6, d6). Heroic (d10, d10, d8, d6). Prodigy (d12, d8, d6, d6).

## Step 3: Tags (Choose 3)

Short phrases describing what you know and have survived. When a Tag applies, step one die up.

* Origin Tag: where you came from
* Role Tag: what you do now
* Wildcard Tag: anything

## Step 4: Flaw

One concrete vulnerability that creates problems in play.

## Step 5: Drive

One active goal. Has an 8-segment Progress Track.

## Step 6: Augments (Choose 2)

Each augment has a starting ability and two upgrades. Choose 2 at creation. Maximum 4 over a campaign. Upgrade or gain new augments at Milestones. A Milestone occurs when a Drive is resolved, a major arc concludes, or both players agree the fiction has shifted enough to warrant growth.

Every augment lists its own costs and constraints. Some are cyberware that increases Burden. Some are companions with Resource Dice. Some are signature items that can be lost or damaged. Some are pure training with no mechanical cost. Read the augment. It tells you what it demands.

See Chapter 3 for the full augment list.

## Step 7: Loadout

Declare your Load Level before the mission. **Do not choose specific items in advance.** During play, when your character reaches for something, tell the table what it is and mark a slot. If it makes sense that you would have it, you do. If you are out of slots, you did not bring it.

| Load Level | Slots | Notes |
| ---------- | ----- | ----- |
| Light | 3 | Fast, inconspicuous. No penalties. |
| Normal | 5 | Standard kit. No penalties. |
| Heavy | 7 | Full loadout. Disadvantage on stealth and mobility Checks. |

Named items on your character sheet (augment gear, signature weapons) do not cost a slot. They are part of you. Programs take 1 slot each.

## Step 8: Connections

You begin with two Connections. These are the people who keep you human in a city that wants to grind you down.

Each Connection has:
* **A name and relationship.** Who they are and what they mean to you.
* **A Willingness Die:** how far they will go for you. Starts at d8 (trusted) or d6 (complicated).
* **A Strain Track:** 4 segments. Tracks how much your life has cost them.

One Connection trusts you. One Connection wants something from you. Both are in danger just by knowing you.

See Chapter 6 for how Strain works.

## Step 9: Tracks

* BODY: 6 boxes. NERVE: 6 boxes.
* Burden: Starts at 1.
* Momentum: 0. Cap: 5.
* Drive Progress: 0/8.

# CHAPTER 4: CONNECTIONS AND STRAIN

## Your Connections

Connections are the people who keep you tethered. They are not resources to be optimized. They are people who care about you in a city that does not.

Every character has at least two Connections. Each has:
* A Willingness Die (how far they will go for you)
* A Strain Track (4 segments. how much your life has cost them)

## How Strain Works

**Strain goes up when:**
* You call on the Connection for help (+1 Strain, always, even on success)
* A consequence table result targets your Connection (+1 Strain)
* A messy mission pulls them into danger (partner narrates how)
* You choose to redirect fallout toward them to shield yourself (+1 Strain, -1 Burden)

**When Strain fills (4/4):**

The Connection confronts you. This is a mandatory scene during Exhale. not a die roll, a conversation. They demand something, threaten to leave, or reveal they have been compromised. You face a Crossroads:

* **Give them what they want**. Strain resets to 0, but you pay a real cost (take +2 Burden, do a job for them, lose a downtime action to their problem).
* **Refuse**. Willingness Die degrades one step. If it was already d6, the Connection is gone permanently.
* **Burn them**. use the Connection as a resource one final time. Automatic Full Hit on one Check, or wipe 3 Burden, or gain critical information. But the Connection is gone forever and the fiction explains why.

**Strain clears from:**
* Downtime action spent on the relationship (describe the scene. this is fiction, not bookkeeping)
* Completing something that directly benefits the Connection
* Taking Burden onto yourself to spare them

## Willingness

When you ask a Connection for help during a mission, roll their Willingness Die. On 5+, they come through. On 1-4, they cannot or will not. not this time, not for this.

After every call, roll the Willingness Die again for degradation: on 1-2, it shrinks one step. Below d6, they stop answering entirely.

## Losing and Gaining Connections

When a Connection is gone (burned, killed, or walked away), that slot is open. You can fill it through fiction: someone new you meet during play, a faction contact, a stranger who helped you when they did not have to. New Connections start at Willingness d6 and Strain 0/4. Trust is earned.

# CHAPTER 5: BURDEN

Burden is a number from 0 to 10. It represents everything pressing down on you: debt, heat, attention, maintenance costs, the consequences of how you live.

## The Burden Scale

| Range | Name | Effects |
| ----- | ---- | ------- |
| 0-2 | **Stable** | No mechanical effects. You have breathing room. Worth protecting. |
| 3-5 | **Strained** | Disadvantage on Checks that require spending money or calling in favors. Connection Willingness rolls for degradation whenever you call on them (in addition to the normal post-call degradation). |
| 6-8 | **Drowning** | Lose 1 downtime action to survival. Partner can invoke your desperation once per session. a free complication with no roll required. You cannot turn down paying work. |
| 9-10 | **Breaking Point** | Lose 2 downtime actions. Cyberware malfunctions unless maintained (1 downtime action per piece). Any further Burden increase forces a Crossroads: something permanent breaks. a Connection, an augment, your safe house, or your cover identity. |

## Burden Goes Up

* **Post-mission Burden Check:** Count mission factors. Each of the following that applies is one factor: anyone died, loud weapons used, corp directly targeted, you were identified, high-security territory, physical evidence left. Roll 1d6 per factor. Each 5+ is +1 Burden.
* **Cyberware installation:** +1 per piece installed after character creation.
* **Economic shocks that escalate.**
* **Narrative events:** safe house lost, Connection burned, faction retaliation.
* **Choosing money over morality.** Some jobs pay because they should not be done.
* **Voluntary:** You can choose to take +1 Burden to acquire something you cannot otherwise afford. a bribe, a rush chrome install, a contact, a safe house. The city notices.

## Burden Goes Down

* **Jobs that pay well:** -1 if pay was financial.
* **Side Gig during downtime:** See Exhale.
* **Faction support:** A faction with high Goodwill shelters you: -1.
* **Sacrifice a Connection:** Burn them (see Chapter 6): -3.
* **Lay Low during downtime:** -1.
* **Do a faction's dirty work:** -1, but tick their Clock forward.
* **Partner support:** If both characters share expenses and neither is above Burden 5, both experience Burden effects as one band lower. This does not change the actual number.

## Economic Shock Table (d8)

Between missions, roll a number of d6 equal to your Burden band: Stable = 0 dice, Strained = 1, Drowning = 2, Breaking Point = 3. Each 5+ triggers a shock. Roll d8:

| d8 | Shock |
| -- | ----- |
| 1 | Rent hike. Lose safe house unless you spend 1 downtime dealing with it. |
| 2 | Chrome lapse. One cyberware augment stops working until maintained (downtime + WITS Check). |
| 3 | Credstick frozen. No purchases or bribes until resolved (NERVE Check or a favor). |
| 4 | Medical debt. Burden +1 or owe a specific NPC. |
| 5 | Fixer's cut. Last job paid less than promised. |
| 6 | Repo. Lose a piece of gear (partner chooses). |
| 7 | Favor called in. Someone wants a job, not credits. Hooks next mission. |
| 8 | The squeeze. Two shocks. Roll twice, ignore 8s. |

# CHAPTER 6: THE RULES

## The Check

Roll your Attribute Die twice. Each die showing 5+ is a Pass.

| Result | Name | What Happens |
| ------ | ---- | ------------ |
| Both Pass | FULL HIT | You get what you wanted. You narrate. |
| One Pass | TWIST | You succeed, but there is a cost. Partner invents a consequence or rolls the table. |
| Neither Pass | MISS | Situation shifts against you. Partner narrates. |

**Tags:** When applicable, step one die up.

**Advantage / Disadvantage:** Favorable fiction steps one die up. Unfavorable steps one down. They cancel.

**SPARK:** Either die shows max face AND the Check is a Full Hit. Bonus effect.

**CATASTROPHE:** Both dice show 1. Fiction gets worse in a new direction.

**PERFECT:** Both dice show max. Extraordinary success.

A Twist can be minor. A ticking clock, a spent resource, a small delay. Friction is enough. Save the dramatic reversals for Misses and Catastrophes.

## Consequence Tables

When a Twist or Miss occurs, the non-acting player can invent a consequence or roll d8 on the appropriate table. The table is a tool, not a mandate. If the fiction provides an obvious answer, use it.

### Combat Consequences (d8)

| d8 | Twist (succeed, but...) | Miss (situation worsens...) |
| -- | ----------------------- | --------------------------- |
| 1 | Position exposed | Flanked or surrounded |
| 2 | Collateral damage (gear, bystander, environment) | Ally hit instead of target |
| 3 | Weapon jams or damaged | Weapon knocked away or destroyed |
| 4 | Reinforcements coming. Tick Threat Clock | Reinforcements arrive now |
| 5 | Enemy counterattack. Partner rolls for them | Enemy presses hard. double attack |
| 6 | Driven from position. Moved one zone | Environment turns hostile |
| 7 | Connection strained. +1 Strain | NPC ally turns, flees, or freezes |
| 8 | Take Minor harm | Take Major harm |

### Social Consequences (d8)

| d8 | Twist | Miss |
| -- | ----- | ---- |
| 1 | They believe you but want something | They see through you |
| 2 | Info gained but someone overheard | You offend them. Future Disadvantage |
| 3 | They agree with conditions | They alert someone you did not want alerted |
| 4 | You revealed more than intended | They manipulate you. 1 NERVE box |
| 5 | Deal is worse than hoped | Deal falls through, opportunity gone |
| 6 | A Connection implicated. +1 Strain | Connection Willingness rolls for degradation |
| 7 | You owe a favor | Bridge burned |
| 8 | Flaw triggered or exposed | Drive complicated. Progress -1 |

### Stealth Consequences (d8)

| d8 | Twist | Miss |
| -- | ----- | ---- |
| 1 | Through but left evidence. Tick Clock | Spotted. combat or chase |
| 2 | Security activates behind you. No going back | Alarm. Threat Clock +2 |
| 3 | Partner or companion exposed | Separated from partner |
| 4 | Gear abandoned or damaged | Trapped |
| 5 | Took too long. Tick Clock | Patrol catches you mid-move |
| 6 | Saw something you should not have | Hidden countermeasure. Minor harm |
| 7 | Disguise strained. Next deception at Disadvantage | Identity compromised |
| 8 | Unexpected NPC present | Target has moved or changed |

### Hacking Consequences (d8)

| d8 | Twist | Miss |
| -- | ----- | ---- |
| 1 | Detection Clock +1 | Detection Clock +2 |
| 2 | Partial access only | Locked out of subsystem |
| 3 | ICE activates | Black ICE. Major NERVE harm |
| 4 | Data encrypted or incomplete | Trace on physical location |
| 5 | Deck overheats. Offline for 1 Exchange | Neural spike. Minor NERVE harm |
| 6 | Silent alarm. someone is watching | Counterattack. jack out or Severe NERVE |
| 7 | Another netrunner in the system | AI fragment notices you |
| 8 | Detection Clock +1 and ICE activates | Full lockdown |

### Exploration Consequences (d8)

| d8 | Twist | Miss |
| -- | ----- | ---- |
| 1 | Route longer. tick time Clock | Lost. waste a scene |
| 2 | Environmental hazard. Minor BODY | Environmental hazard. Major BODY |
| 3 | Found it but guarded/trapped/unstable | Location picked clean or occupied |
| 4 | Used up resources. Lose a loadout slot | Gear lost or destroyed |
| 5 | Unexpected NPC | Ambush. enemy acts first |

| 7 | Gear stressed. damaged, limited use | Gear destroyed |
| 8 | Find something unexpected. Roll Oracle | Path forward requires sacrifice |

## Momentum

Momentum is your meta-currency. It covers preparation, desperation, and everything in between.

**Earn 1 Momentum when:**
* You play your Flaw and it costs you.
* You take a risk pursuing your Drive.
* The table agrees you did something remarkable.
* Your Prep Check succeeds (Full Hit: +2 Momentum. Twist: +1 Momentum).

**Cap: 5. Resets to 0 each session.**

**Spend Momentum:**

| Cost | Effect |
| ---- | ------ |
| 1 | **Boost**. step one die up on your next Check |
| 1 | **Flashback**. declare something you prepared earlier. Narrate it. |
| 1 | **Mitigate**. shift a Consequence Table result down one row |
| 1 | **Activate**. use a single-use item from your loadout |
| 2 | **Defy the Odds**. reroll a Miss |
| 2 | **Narrative Authority**. introduce a useful fact into the fiction |
| 3 | **Refuse to Fall**. when Broken, stand back up at 1 box (once per session) |

### Prep

Before the first Explore scene, each character makes one Prep Check using any relevant Attribute and Tag. This represents the time between taking the job and walking into danger.

| Result | Momentum Earned |
| ------ | --------------- |
| Full Hit | +2 Momentum |
| Twist | +1 Momentum + a complication (partner narrates what went sideways during prep) |
| Miss | +0. Your preparation failed or was wasted. |

Prep feeds directly into the Momentum pool. There is no separate prep currency. What you earned from preparation is the same resource you spend on flashbacks, boosts, and desperation moves. Manage it accordingly.

## Harm

BODY (6 boxes) and NERVE (6 boxes).

Minor = 1 box. Major = 2. Severe = 3.

### Recovery

| Method | Effect |
| ------ | ------ |
| Rest (downtime scene, safe location) | Clear 2 boxes across both tracks |
| Full Recovery (two downtime actions + safe location) | Clear all boxes |
| Field Treatment (partner makes a WITS Check) | Full Hit: clear 2 on one track. Twist: clear 1. Miss: 1 Minor harm instead. |

No quick recovery during missions. Harm sticks until you find safety.

### Broken

When either harm track fills, you are Broken. Choose:

| Choice | Outcome |
| ------ | ------- |
| Blaze of Glory | Automatic Full Hit + Spark. Then you are out of the scene. |
| Hold On | Down. Partner stabilizes you. Gain a Scar. |
| Burn Permanent | Sacrifice something forever (Attribute steps down, Tag lost, Connection severed, augment destroyed). Back up at 1 box. |

### Scars

Inverse Tags. step a die DOWN when the fiction triggers them. Partner can invoke a Scar once per session.

## Clocks and Progress Tracks

A Clock is a circle divided into segments. They are visual, shared, and tracked openly.

Threat Clocks count toward something bad. When full, the threat arrives.

Progress Tracks count toward something you want. When full, the goal is achieved.

**When to create a Clock:** Create a Clock whenever a threat or goal emerges that will take more than one Check to resolve. Either player can propose a Clock at any time. Name it, choose a size, and put it where both players can see it. If a Clock becomes irrelevant, cross it out. There is no limit on active Clocks, but if you have more than four or five going at once, some of them probably do not matter enough to track.

| Clock Size | Use For |
| ---------- | ------- |
| 4 segments | Short-term, urgent (building collapse, alarm, escape window) |
| 6 segments | Standard (security response, hacking a system, earning faction trust) |
| 8 segments | Long-term (faction plans, Drive resolution, campaign-scale threats) |

**Ticking:**
* Full Hit toward a Progress Track: +2 segments
* Twist toward a Progress Track: +1 segment
* Miss: +1 on a related Threat Clock
* Narrative events, Consequence Tables, and Faction Turns can tick Clocks at any time

For two-player games, use smaller Clocks (4 instead of 6) for mission-level threats. Two characters generate fewer actions per scene, so Clocks should fill faster to maintain pressure.

# CHAPTER 7: AUGMENTS

Augments are structured abilities with three tiers: a starting ability and two upgrades. Everything that gives your character a special mechanical capability. cyberware, training, companions, signature gear. is an augment.

Choose 2 at creation. Maximum 4 over a campaign. Upgrade or gain new augments at Milestones. A Milestone occurs when a Drive is resolved, a major arc concludes, or both players agree the fiction has shifted enough to warrant growth.

Each augment lists its own costs. Some increase Burden when installed. Some come with companion Resource Dice that degrade. Some are physical items that can be taken from you. Some cost nothing but require fictional justification.

### Augments and Burden

Cyberware augments increase your Burden by 1 when installed after character creation. Cyberware chosen at creation does not increase Burden. you have already absorbed the cost. Cyberware requires proprietary maintenance and firmware subscriptions. The corps sell you the upgrade. Then they charge you to keep it running.

### Companions

Companion augments follow specific narration rules: the owning player decides the companion's actions, but the partner rolls and narrates consequences. When a companion acts independently, the partner has full narrative authority. Companions have a Resource Die (Loyalty, Power, or Stability) that can shrink through fiction. When a companion is stressed, endangered, or neglected, roll the Resource Die. On 1-2, it steps down. Below d6, the companion leaves, breaks, or turns against you.

# CHAPTER 8: COMBAT

## The Exchange

An Exchange is one round of combat. Each Exchange follows this sequence:

1. Describe the situation.
2. Declare intent (partner determines enemy action via NPC Action Table).
3. Roll simultaneously.
4. Resolve.
5. New Exchange.

## Attack Sequence

1. Check (Attribute x2, 5+ passes, Tag steps one up).
2. Full Hit: weapon die x2, take higher. Twist: weapon die x1. Miss: no damage.
3. Compare to thresholds + armor. Mark harm boxes.

### Damage Thresholds

Base thresholds: 4 (Major) and 8 (Severe). Damage below the Major threshold is Minor. Armor raises both thresholds.

| Armor | Bonus | Major At | Severe At |
| ----- | ----- | -------- | --------- |
| None | +0 | 4 | 8 |
| Light | +2 | 6 | 10 |
| Medium | +3 | 7 | 11 |
| Heavy | +4 | 8 | 12 |

When damage bounces off armor: change tactics. Hack their systems. EMP their chrome. Flank. Use the environment. Not every fight is won with bigger guns.

## Weapons

| Weapon | Die | Tags |
| ------ | --- | ---- |
| Fists / improvised | d6 | Close Only |
| Knife / mono-blade | d6 | Concealable, Close Only |
| Light pistol | d8 | Concealable, Ammo |
| Heavy pistol | d8 | Loud, Ammo |
| SMG | d8 | Loud, Ammo, Area |
| Shotgun | d10 | Loud, Messy, Slow, Ammo |
| Assault rifle | d10 | Loud, Ammo |
| Sniper rifle | d12 | Loud, Precise, Slow, Ammo |
| Frag grenade | d10 | Area, Loud, Messy |

### Weapon Tags

| Tag | Effect |
| --- | ------ |
| Concealable | Hidden unless searched |
| Loud | Everyone nearby knows. May increase Burden. |
| Messy | Collateral damage |
| Precise | On Spark, choose which harm track |
| Area | Hits everyone in a zone |
| Slow | Reload/recharge between uses |
| Ammo | On a Twist, you can be running low. On a Miss, you can be out. Resupply through fiction or a loadout slot. |
| Unreliable | On Catastrophe, breaks or jams |
| Brutal | Severe harm also inflicts a Scar |

| EMP | Disable one cyberware or powered armor for one Exchange |
| Shock | Deals NERVE harm instead of BODY |
| Monofilament | Ignores armor below Heavy |

### Gear in Play

Gear does not have quality dice or degradation tracks. Gear works until the fiction says it does not. Consequence table results like "weapon jams," "gear damaged," or "gear destroyed" handle wear and breakdowns. Augments that are signature items are protected from destruction by consequence tables (they can still be lost, stolen, or damaged through major fiction events).

When a consequence table result damages or destroys your gear, describe what happens. Damaged gear still works but gains a fictional limitation (scope cracked, barrel warped, battery dying). Destroyed gear is gone. Replace it during downtime or through fiction.

### Damaging NPCs

NPCs use simplified harm. They do not have separate BODY/NERVE tracks or damage thresholds. When you deal damage to an NPC, compare the result to their Capacity:

* **Gangers (1 hit):** Any successful damage takes them out.
* **Professionals (4 boxes), Elites (6 boxes), Bosses (8+ boxes):** Mark boxes equal to the harm severity. Minor = 1, Major = 2, Severe = 3. When their boxes fill, they are out. The fiction determines what "out" means: dead, unconscious, fleeing, surrendering.

Armor works the same for NPCs as for PCs. it raises the damage thresholds.

# APPENDIX A: QUICK REFERENCE

## The Check

Attribute Die x2. 5+ = Pass. Both = Full Hit. One = Twist. Neither = Miss.

Spark: max face on Full Hit. Catastrophe: double 1. Perfect: double max.

## Attack

Full Hit: weapon x2 take higher. Twist: weapon x1. Miss: none.

Thresholds: below Major = Minor (1 box), Major+ = Major (2 boxes), Severe+ = Severe (3 boxes). Armor raises both thresholds.

| Armor | Major At | Severe At |
| ----- | -------- | --------- |
| None | 4 | 8 |
| Light (+2) | 6 | 10 |
| Medium (+3) | 7 | 11 |
| Heavy (+4) | 8 | 12 |

## Burden

0-10 scale. Bands: Stable (0-2), Strained (3-5), Drowning (6-8), Breaking Point (9-10).

Post-mission: count factors, roll d6 per factor, 5+ = +1 Burden.

Between missions: roll shock dice by band (0/1/2/3). Each 5+ = economic shock.

## Connections

Willingness Die + Strain Track (4 segments). Strain fills = mandatory Crossroads scene.

## Momentum

Earn: Flaw, Drive, remarkable, Prep Checks. Spend: Boost (1), Flashback (1), Mitigate (1), Activate (1), Defy Odds (2), Authority (2), Refuse to Fall (3). Cap 5.

Hook → Explore → Leverage (SWAP) → Ignite → Exhale. 75-105 min.

## Exhale Sequence

1. Mission Burden Check. 2. Economic Shocks. 3. Connection Strain. 4. Faction Turn. 5. Pay. 6. Downtime. 7. Drive Progress. 8. Flags.

## Downtime Menu

Side Gig, Recover, Tend Connection, Maintain Chrome, Work the Streets, Advance, Lay Low, Redirect Fallout. 2 actions minus Burden tax.

## Die Ladder

d6 → d8 → d10 → d12. Cannot exceed d12. Cannot drop below d6.

# APPENDIX B: PLAY EXAMPLES

The following is one continuous mission played across a full HELIX session. Alex and Sam are the two players. Alex drives first.

## HOOK

Alex (driving): "Deckard calls. Data retrieval from a drowned server room in the Drain. Double the usual rate."

Sam (pushing back): "Why double? What is Deckard not saying?"

Alex: Let us ask the Oracle. *Rolls Action + Theme.* "Conceal... Data." He knows what is on that drive and he is not telling us.

Sam: He is playing us. Let us go.

That is the Hook. Under five minutes. A job and a seed of trouble.

## EXPLORE. THE NOODLE SHOP

Alex (driving, framing the scene): "Ma Lin's noodle shop, Sector 12. Zero and Kira are at the counter. It smells like fish sauce and engine grease. Ma Lin is wiping down the same bowl she has been wiping for ten minutes. The question is: what does she know about the drowned server room?"

Sam (pushing back, voicing Ma Lin): "You want to go down there? Three levels under? The Canal Rats pulled a body out of there last month." *She stops wiping the bowl.* "They said the lights were still on."

Alex (as Kira): "Lights mean power. Servers might still be running. Zero, can you get us in?"

Sam (as Zero): "I grew up down there. I know the route." *I am making a WITS Check. My WITS is d10 and my Tag 'Grew Up in the Flooded Undercity' steps one die to d12.* Rolls d10 and d12. Gets 7 and 3. One pass. Twist.

Sam (now pushing back as the world): so Zero knows the route, but what is the cost? *Rolls d8 on Social Consequence Table. Gets 5: "Deal worse than hoped."* "You remember the way. But the Canal Rats started charging tolls last month. They own the main stairwell now. Double rate or find another way."

Alex: "We do not have double. But Kira is not paying a toll to walk into a deathtrap." *Turns to Sam.* "I want to cut. Next scene: the canal entrance on the west side, looking for the smuggler's tunnel."

Sam: "Good. Cut."

## EXPLORE. THE FLOODED CANAL

Alex (framing): "West canal, Sector 12. Dark. Water up to mid-thigh and rising. Kira has a flashlight. Zero is navigating. The question is: can they find the side tunnel before the tide comes in?"

Sam (pushing back): "The water is getting warmer. That is a bad sign. it means the reclamation pumps are cycling. You have maybe twenty minutes." *Turns to Alex.* "What does Kira think about tight spaces?"

Alex (as Kira): "She hates them. But she hates owing people more. We go." *EDGE Check. My EDGE is d8. No Tag applies.* Rolls d8 twice. Gets 5 and 2. One pass. Twist.

Sam: "You find the tunnel. It is narrow and it stinks but it is there." *Does not roll the table. the obvious dramatic answer is clear.* "Your comm unit shorted out in the water. No signal down here. If something goes wrong, nobody is coming."

Alex: "That is the end of Explore. We are at the server room entrance. We know the route, we have no comms, and the Canal Rats are watching the main exit."

Sam: "Clocks?"

Alex: "Ferrik Arrives: 6 segments, starting at 0. Retrieve Data: 4 segments. Let us do Leverage."

Notice: Sam pushed back twice. once with the Consequence Table (Canal Rat tolls), once with pure fiction (comm unit shorted). Both are valid. Sam also asked a character question ("What does Kira think about tight spaces?") that had nothing to do with the Check. it was just good storytelling. Pushing back is not only mechanical. It is emotional.

## LEVERAGE. THE SWAP

Sam (now driving. was pushing back through Hook and Explore): "Server room. Knee-deep in black water. Flickering emergency lights. Rows of dead server racks like tombstones. Kira spots the dead drop. a magnetic case on a rack in the back corner."

Alex (now pushing back): "Zero jacks in to check if the data is intact?"

Sam: "She does. And while she is connected  - " *Sam leans forward.* "Boots on the stairwell. Military cadence. That is Ferrik." *Ticks the Ferrik Arrives Clock to 3.* "But that is not the Leverage."

Alex: "Oh no. What is it?"

Sam: "Zero pulls up the file index to verify. And she sees what is on the drive. It is not corporate financials. It is medical records. Solvane's experimental chrome testing program. Patient numbers. Procedure logs. And one of the test subjects listed is her sister."

Alex: *Long pause.* "Okay. Okay."

Sam (as Zero): "Zero is frozen. She is staring at the screen and her hands are shaking."

Alex (now pushing back, as Kira): "Kira does not know what Zero just saw. She is watching the stairwell, hand on her pistol. 'Zero. Grab the drive. We do not have time.'"

Sam (as Zero): "Zero does not move."

That is the Leverage. The mission just became personal. Nobody planned this moment. It emerged from the collision between Zero's Drive and the fiction.

## IGNITE. THE STANDOFF

Alex (pushing back, as Kira): "Kira grabs Zero's shoulder. 'We need to go. NOW.'" *NERVE Check to snap her out of it. NERVE is d8.* Rolls d8 twice. Gets 6 and 4. Both pass. Full Hit.

Alex: "Kira's voice cuts through. Zero unplugs the drive and pockets it. She whispers: 'My sister is on this drive, Kira.'"

Sam (driving): "Before Kira can answer. Sergeant Hollis steps through the main entrance. Four operatives behind her. Rifle leveled. 'Put the drive on the ground and step back. This does not need to be ugly.'"

*Both players pause. This is a decision point.*

Sam: "This is a Crossroads. Fight, run, or talk?"

Alex (as Kira): "How many operatives?"

Sam: "Four plus Hollis. Professional tier, d8 Checks, SMGs, light armor. Hollis is Elite, d10, assault rifle, medium armor. Fighting is survivable but ugly."

Alex: "Kira is a former Ferrik agent. She knows how these teams work. She knows Hollis is following orders, not hunting for sport." *Thinks.* "She steps forward. 'Sergeant. Do you know what you are here to collect?'"

Sam: "That is a NERVE Check. Hollis is not friendly."

Alex: *NERVE d8. Tag 'I Can Read a Room Before the Door Opens' steps one die to d10.* Rolls d10 and d8. Gets 9 and 3. One pass. Twist.

Sam (determining the Twist): "Hollis lowers her rifle an inch. She is listening. That is the success." *Rolls d8 on Combat Consequence Table. Gets 7: "Connection strained."* "But one of her operatives is already reporting in. Ferrik command now knows the team made contact. Clock ticks to 5. You have minutes."

Alex: "Kira turns to Zero. 'Show her.'"

Sam (as Zero): "Zero pulls up the medical records on the terminal. Hollis reads. Her face changes."

Sam (driving, voicing Hollis): "'Get out. Side tunnel. I will tell them you were gone when we arrived.'" *Hollis looks at the screen one more time.* "'This needs to reach someone who can do something with it. Not Ferrik. Not Solvane.'"

Sam: "She is letting you go. Clock is at 5. Run."

Notice: Alex was pushing back but still made the big character choice (negotiating instead of fighting). Pushing back does not mean passive. You always play your own character. The roles determine who frames the world, not who makes decisions.

## EXHALE

Sam: "Okay. Post-mission. Let us count Burden factors."

Alex: "Did anyone die? No. Loud weapons? No, we talked our way out. Corp directly targeted? Yes, this is Solvane's data. Plus one."

Sam: "Identified? Hollis saw our faces. Plus one. High-security territory? No, the Drain is low-security. Physical evidence? We did not leave anything. Net total: two factors."

Alex: *Rolls 2d6. Gets 3 and 5.* "One 4-plus. Burden goes from 1 to 2."

Sam: "Burden 2 is Stable band. No shock dice this time."

Alex: "Connection Strain. Did anything hit our Connections?"

Sam: "The Consequence Table result was 'Connection strained'. that was Hollis, not one of ours. But Deckard brokered this job and Ferrik knows. I would say +1 Strain on Deckard."

Alex: "Agreed. Deckard is now at 1/4 Strain."

Sam: "Faction Turn. Solvane was affected." *Rolls d6. Gets 4: Advance.* "Solvane's Dependency Protocol Clock ticks from 2 to 3. They are making progress."

Alex: *Rolls d6 for Ferrik. Gets 3: Pressure. Rolls d6 for target: gets 5. targets a player Connection.* "Ferrik leans on one of our Connections. Which makes more sense?"

Sam: "Deckard. He brokered the job."

Alex: "+1 Strain on Deckard. He is now at 2/4."

Sam: "Pay. Sato pays through a dead drop. Financial. Burden drops by one."

Alex: "Burden goes from 2 to 1."

Sam: "Downtime. Two actions each. I am taking Recover for Kira. clear 2 harm boxes. And Lay Low. Burden drops from 1 to 0."

Alex: "Zero takes Work the Streets. WITS Check to gather intel on the sister lead." *Rolls. Full Hit.* "Two questions: Where was the Solvane testing facility? And is Zero's sister still alive?"

Sam: "The facility was in Sector 1. the Pinnacle. Corporate territory. And..." *Rolls Oracle: Yes, but.* "She is alive. But she is not in the facility anymore. She was moved."

Alex: "Second downtime action: Tend Connection on Deckard. Clear 2 Strain. He is back to 0/4. Zero buys him a drink and tells him she is sorry for the heat."

Sam: "Drive Progress. Zero found her sister's name in the medical records. That is Significant. tick 2 segments. Drive is now at 2/8."

Alex: "Kira's Drive. 'Find who set up the ambush'. no progress this mission. Still 0/8."

Sam: "Flags. I want to pursue the sister lead next session. Challenge Zero's trust in adults."

Alex: "I want a scene with Lena Stahl. Ferrik internal affairs. And put Kira's drinking in the spotlight. I will drive first next time."

Sam: "Done."
